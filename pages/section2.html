The Brooklyn Waterfront: A Virtual Tour Highlighting Its Industrial Heritage

The study focuses on developing material for a course using the Brooklyn waterfront as a resource. Research findings were used to create a virtual tour prototype illustrating the transformation of the Brooklyn waterfront from the time of its first native inhabitants to Dutch settlement, industrialization, deindustrialization and current gentrification. The history and transformation of six neighborhoods on the waterfront; Gravesend, Red Hook, Coney Island, Dumbo, Williamsburg, and Greenpoint are illustrated using virtual tour technology. The importance of Brooklyn’s maritime economy, the forces contributing to its decline and the socio-economic impact that resulted. The resurgence of the Brooklyn waterfront, including housing and other real estate development, parks and retail and the water itself, are also illustrated.

Audio Amplifier

An amplifier is an electronic device used to increase the magnitude of voltage/current/power of an input signal. It takes in a weak electrical signal/waveform and reproduces a similar stronger waveform at the output by using an external power source. The power of the input signal is increased to a level high enough to drive loads of output devices like speakers, headphones, RF transmitters etc. The input signal to a power amplifier needs to be above a certain threshold. So instead of directly passing the raw audio/RF signal to the power amplifier, it is first pre-amplified using current/voltage amplifiers and is sent as input to the power amp after making necessary modifications.

The objective of this experiment was to create and build a PCB audio amplifier using an IC’s TDA2050. The TDA2050 is a great sounding chip amplifier with lots of power. In this project, I went through the amplifier design process and I build one.
I calculated the voltage and current requirements of the power supply. Then, I learnt how to find the right values for all of the components in the circuit. Also, how to change the gain value, PCB design and wiring the amplifier inside an enclosure.

Controller Circuit (EET 2271 see there)

This controller was made by employing three fundamental circuits: a delay circuit, a triggering circuit, and an output circuit. This project is an application of all the skills obtained from EET courses to conceptualize, design, simulate, build and test a controller circuit; and to utilize the components involved in designing systematic delays to properly build a switching system.
The controller has three systems: A,B and C, where it must be switched on in the following sequence: System A turns on in the beginning of the experiment while the other two remain off. After 2 seconds, system B is activated while system A remains ON. After 3 seconds, system C is activated while system A and B remains ON.
